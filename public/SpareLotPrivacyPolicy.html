Last updated: July 12, 2024

SpareLot exists to help build connections between people and make communities more open and inclusive. A fundamental part of earning the trust of our users means being clear about how we use your information and protecting your human right to privacy.

This Privacy Policy describes how SpareLot LLC and its affiliates (“we,” “us,” or “SpareLot”), process personal information in relation to your use of the SpareLot Platform. Please follow the links and review the supplemental information describing how we process personal information.

Privacy Policy

1. Personal Information We Collect

The personal information we collect from you, directly or indirectly, will depend on how you interact with us and with our Service. From time to time, we may collect personal information not specifically listed in this Privacy Policy, which we will use as described in this Privacy Policy or as otherwise explained at or before the time of collection. The sources from which we collect personal information include the following:

Information you provide to us. Personal information you may provide to us or our service providers through the Service or otherwise includes:

  • Contact Data: Such as your first and last name, email address, mailing addresses, zip code, and phone number.
  • Account Data: Such as the username and password that you set to establish an online account on the Service, identity document information (e.g., information from a passport or driver’s license), client type, contact data, and any other information you choose to include with your account, such as your account preferences.
  • Biometric Data: Which includes biometric information and/or identifiers in the form of facial geometry extracted from images of your face in the photos and/or identity documents that you provide to third party identity verification services that we may use from time to time. We use these identity verification services to verify your identity and prevent fraud when you register for the Service. We do not collect or store biometric data.
  • User Content: Which includes your comments, feedback, photos, videos, audio, chats, and messages that you post to areas of the Service visible to other Service users or in messages to other Service users.
  • Payment Data: Which includes the payment card data, contact details, and in certain cases, date of birth, required to process payments made through the Service, as well as transaction history. Your payment transactions are processed by third party payment processors, such as Stripe, which collect your personal information as described below under How we share your personal information. If you make payments from a bank account connected to your Stripe account, we collect your Stripe account ID, bank name, last 4 digits of bank account number, and routing number.
  • Communications Data: Such as your communications with us or the Service and data about those communications and interactions, including when you contact us through the Service or social media, you contact support, or otherwise communicate with us. When you communicate with us through social media, this will include your social media handle. When you communicate with us via text messages, this may include your cell phone number, your mobile carrier's name, and the date, time, and content of your messages. Communications data does not include user content.
  • Promotions Data: Including information you share when you choose to enter a contest, promotion, sweepstakes, rewards program, or complete a survey.

Third-party sources. We may combine personal information we receive from you with personal information (e.g., demographic and contact information) that we obtain from other sources, such as:

  • Data Providers: Such as information services and data licensors.
  • Public Sources: Such as government agencies, public records, social media platforms, and other publicly available sources.
  • Marketing Partners: Such as joint marketing partners and event co-sponsors.
  • Third-party Services: Such as social media services, that you use to log into your Service account or to which you connect to share content on those services. This data may include your username, profile picture and other information associated with your account on that third-party service that is made available to us based on your account settings on that service.

Automatic collection. When you access or interact with the Service or our communications, we, our service providers, and our advertising partners may automatically log, monitor, and record information about you, your computer or mobile device, and your interaction over time with the Service, our communications, and other online services. This information includes:

  • Device Data: Such as your computer or mobile device's operating system type and version, manufacturer and model, browser type, screen resolution, RAM and disk size, CPU usage, device type (e.g., phone, tablet), IP address, unique identifiers (including identifiers used for advertising purposes), language settings, mobile device carrier, radio/network information (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, LTE, 3G), general location information (e.g., city, state) that can be derived from your IP address.
  • Usage Data: Such as pages or screens you viewed, your search terms, how long you spent on a page or screen, the website you visited before browsing to the Service, navigation paths between pages or screens, information about your activity on a page or screen, access times and duration of access, whether you have opened our emails or clicked links within them, and other functional information on Service performance (like diagnostics and crash logs).
  • Precise Geolocation: Location of your device when you grant the Service access to it in your device's settings.
  • Other Device Data: We collect from your device when you grant the Service access to it, such as audio collected from your device’s microphone to enable voice commands; motion data collected from your device’s gyroscope, accelerometer, or other sensors; and photos or video accessed from your device’s photos or camera.

Cookies and similar technologies. We, our service providers, and our advertising partners use the following technologies to facilitate some of the automatic collection of personal information described above, including to enable Service functionality, analytics on how it is used, and interest-based advertising:

  • Cookies: Which are text files that websites store on a visitor’s device to uniquely identify the visitor’s browser or to store information or settings in the browser for the purpose of tracking user activity and patterns, helping the visitor navigate between pages efficiently, remembering preferences and whether a visitor is logged in, and improving the visitor’s browsing experience. Some cookies store information only during a user session, others persist in your browser and collect data from multiple sessions.
  • Pixels: (Also known as web beacons or clear GIFs), which are embedded in code and/or invisibly as image files within web pages or HTML formatted emails and used to demonstrate that a webpage or email was accessed or opened, or that certain content was viewed or clicked, at a specific date and time using a specific device, typically to compile statistics about usage of websites and the success of marketing campaigns.
  • Session Replay: A technology that uses third-party software code to record users’ interactions with the Service in a manner that allows us to watch and analyze visual reconstructions of them. The replays include users’ clicks/taps, mouse movements, scrolls, and keystrokes/touches during those sessions. These replays help us diagnose usability problems and identify areas for improvement.
  • Software Development Kits: Which are used to incorporate third party computer code into our mobile app that allows our third party service providers or advertising partners to collect data directly from our app for a variety of purposes, including to provide us with analytics regarding use of our app, to integrate with social media, add features or functionality to our app, or to facilitate and measure the effectiveness of interest-based advertising.
  • Embedded Scripts: Which use software code temporarily downloaded to your device to collect information about your interactions with the Service such as the links you click on.
  • Other Technologies: We may also use device identifiers, local storage (including HTML 5), html modifiers, and different types of caching to help us understand the devices and users who access the Service. Those methods include device identifiers that are either hardware-based or software-based, persistent, or non-persistent, and which may identify either a device or a software module within a device (such as a web browser).
